Abstract
Adjuvant endocrine therapy (AET) is the treatment of choice for early-stage estrogen receptor alpha (ERÎ±)-positive breast cancer (BC). However, almost 40% of tamoxifen-treated cases display no response or a partial response to AET, thus increasing the need for new treatment options and strong predictors of the therapeutic response of patients at high risk of relapse. In addition to ERÎ±, BC research has focused on ERÎ²1 and ERÎ²2 (isoforms of ERÎ²), the second ER isotype. At present, the impact of ERÎ² isoforms on ERÎ±-positive BC prognosis and treatment remains elusive. In the present study, we established clones of MCF7 cells constitutively expressing human ERÎ²1 or ERÎ²2 and investigated their role in the response of MCF7 cells to antiestrogens [4-hydroxytamoxifen (OHÎ¤) and fulvestrant (ICI182,780)] and retinoids [all-trans retinoic acid (ATRA)]. We show that, compared to MCF7 cells, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells were sensitized and desensitized, respectively, to the antiproliferative effect of the antiestrogens, ATRA and their combination and to the cytocidal effect of the combination of OHT and ATRA. Analysis of the global transcriptional changes upon OHTâ€“ATRA combinatorial treatment revealed uniquely regulated genes associated with anticancer effects in MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells and cancer-promoting effects in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells. Our data are favorable to ERÎ²1 being a marker of responsiveness and ERÎ²2 being a marker of resistance of MCF7 cells to antiestrogens alone and in combination with ATRA.

Keywords: breast cancer; ERÎ²1; ERÎ²2; prognostic markers; tamoxifen; ICI182,780; ATRA; gene expression; cell proliferation; cell death

1. Introduction
Depending on expression of estrogen receptor alpha (ERÎ±), progesterone receptor (PR) and human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2), breast cancer (BC) is classified as ERÎ±-positive (ERÎ±+/PR+/HER2Â±), EGFR2-overexpressing (ERÎ±-/PR-/HER2+) and triple negative (ERÎ±-/PR-/HER2-), with â‰ˆ70% of all BC cases categorized as ERÎ±-positive [1]. ERÎ±-positive BC is treated with adjuvant endocrine therapy (AET), which comprises selective ERÎ± modulators (SERMs, e.g., tamoxifen), selective ERÎ± degraders (SERDs, e.g., ICI182,780) and aromatase inhibitors (AIs, e.g., anastrazole), with tamoxifen for five years considered a treatment of choice for a large fraction of early-stage ERÎ±-positive BC cases [2]. However, approx. 40% of tamoxifen-treated ERÎ±-positive BC cases display no response or a partial response to AET, and disease recurrence 20 years after onset of AET following primary surgery is a fairly common outcome [3]. Since the recurrent disease is frequently more aggressive and/or metastatic, new treatment options and relevant strong predictors of the therapeutic response of those patients at intermediate or high risk of relapse are urgently needed.

Retinoids, such as all-trans retinoic acid (ATRA, the major active form of vitamin A), inhibit proliferation and viability of tamoxifen-sensitive and tamoxifen-resistant ERÎ±-positive BC cells and the growth of experimental ERÎ±-positive breast tumors, but they reportedly have no effect on the survival of breast cancer patients whether administered alone or in combination with tamoxifen [4,5]. The anticancer activity of retinoids is mediated by three retinoic acid receptor isotypes (RARÎ±, RARÎ² and RARÎ³) and three retinoid X receptor isotypes (RXRÎ±, RXRÎ² and RXRÎ³) [6]. ATRA, in particular, is known to preferentially bind RARs and stimulate formation of RARâ€“RXR heterodimers, which bind to retinoic acid response elements (RAREs) of retinoid target genes to regulate gene expression [6]. RARÎ± is preferentially expressed in ERÎ±-positive BC cells, including MCF7 cells, a classical model of early-stage ERÎ±-positive BC. In these cells, estrogen-stimulated ERÎ± is known to induce RARÎ±, which then can cooperate with chromatin-bound ERÎ± to regulate estrogen-dependent cell proliferation and gene expression in concertation with other ERÎ± cofactors [7]. Conversely, ATRA-stimulated RARÎ± inhibits ERÎ±-dependent cell proliferation and gene expression by competing with ERÎ± for binding to adjacent or overlapping DNA regulatory elements and/or by interfering with ERÎ± cofactor recruitment [7,8,9]. ATRA interference with ERÎ± transcription complexes downregulates the expression of ERÎ± and several estrogen-responsive genes, including PR and pS2, and in combination with 4-hydroxytamoxifen (OHT), an active metabolite of tamoxifen, synergistically inhibits proliferation of MCF7 cells [10,11,12]. It has been reported that ATRA treatment inhibits proliferation and viability of MCF7 cells by decreasing IGF-1 expression and secretion and by downregulating EGFR signaling and IRS-1 expression and signaling through the IRS-1/PI3kinase/AKT pathway [13,14,15,16].

OHT is known to act as estrogen agonist or antagonist through ERÎ±, depending on cell-specific recruitment of coactivators or corepressors to genes that regulate key cell responses to the drug, including inhibition of cell proliferation [17,18]. It has been reported that in MCF7 cells, OHT upregulates the expression of a set of genes marginally induced by estrogen, including genes overexpressed in ERÎ±-positive tumors of patients that suffered tumor relapse following primary surgery and adjuvant treatment with tamoxifen. Moreover, it was observed that introduction of ERÎ²1, the second ER isotype, inhibited OHT-induced upregulation of these genes in MCF7 cells and modulated the expression of unique as well as ERÎ±-regulated genes in a manner that favors inhibition of cell proliferation and/or induction of apoptosis [19,20]. There are five isoforms of ERÎ² (ERÎ²1-5), of which only ERÎ²1, the product of the full transcript of ERÎ² gene, has high affinity for estradiol and tamoxifen [21,22]. ERÎ²1 and ERÎ²2 are the isoforms most frequently expressed in BC [23]. ERÎ²2 can modulate gene expression independently and/or in association with ERÎ±, ERÎ²1 or other transcription factors [22,24,25,26]. ERÎ²1 is associated with an improved response of ERÎ±-positive BC to tamoxifen, although not in all clinical studies [27,28]. We have previously reported that ERÎ²2 is associated with poor prognosis in ERÎ±-negative BC and with higher risk of late relapse of AÎ•Î¤-treated early-stage ERÎ±-positive BC, while ERÎ²1 is associated with lower risk of early relapse of AET-treated early-stage ERÎ±-positive BC [29,30]. In the present report, we compare the effects of OHT and ICI182,780 (ICI) used separately or in combination with ATRA on proliferation, viability and gene expression of wild-type MCF7 cells and clones thereof made to stably express ERÎ²1 or ERÎ²2. We show that ATRA potentiated the inhibitory effect of OHT and ICI on the proliferation and viability of wild-type MCF7 cells and that ERÎ²1 sensitized and ERÎ²2 desensitized the cells to inhibitory effects of OHT and ICI. Global gene expression analysis identified gene expression patterns associated with the response of ERÎ²1- or ERÎ²2-expressing MCF7 cells to the combination of OHT with ATRA, which suggest that ERÎ²1 and ERÎ²2 could function as predictors of therapeutic and adverse responses, respectively, of early-stage ERÎ±-positive BC to antiestrogens alone or in combination with ATRA.

2. Results
2.1. Establishment of MCF7 Clones Stably Expressing Human ERÎ²1 or ERÎ²2 and Assessment of Their Effect on ERÎ± Gene Expression and Transcriptional Activity
In order to investigate the role of ERÎ² isoforms in the response of breast cancer cells to antiestrogen treatment, we established clones of MCF7 cells constitutively expressing human ERÎ²1 or ERÎ²2. After initial screening, two clones were selected to be further used in the present study. Figure 1A,B show significantly higher specific expression of ERÎ²1 and ERÎ²2 mRNA in the MCF7-ERÎ²1 and the MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells, respectively. Western blot analysis confirmed the expression of ERÎ²1 and Î•RÎ²2 isoforms in MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells, respectively (Figure 1C,D). Next, we assessed the effect of ERÎ²1 and ERÎ²2 expression on ERÎ± expression levels. ERÎ± mRNA levels were significantly decreased in both MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells as compared to MCF7-WT cells (Figure 1E). Similarly, ERÎ²1- and ERÎ²2-expressing MCF7 cells displayed lower estrogen binding capacity as compared to WT cells (Figure 1F). In addition, ERÎ± protein levels in both MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 were reduced as compared to MCF7-WT cells (Figure 1G). Taken together, our data demonstrate that expression of either ERÎ²1 or ERÎ²2 downregulates ERÎ± expression in MCF7 cells.

To further extend our observations, we addressed the effect of ERÎ²1 and ERÎ²2 expression in MCF7 cells on the transcriptional regulation of the pS2 gene, an ERE-dependent estrogen target [31]. pS2 mRNA levels were significantly induced by estradiol in MCF7-WT and MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells (â‰ˆ4.0- and 3.6-fold, respectively), whereas less pronounced induction (2.3-fold) was observed in cells expressing ERÎ²2 (Figure 1H). Expression of pS2 mRNA was not induced upon treatment with OHT and ICI. Importantly, significant induction of pS2 mRNA was observed upon treatment of MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells with a combination of the ERÎ±-specific antagonist MPP [32] and the ERÎ²1-specific agonist DPN [33], suggesting that ERÎ²1 is transcriptionally active in the absence of ERÎ± activation.

2.2. ERÎ²1 Sensitized and ERÎ²2 Desensitized, Respectively, MCF7 Cells to the Antiproliferative Effect of OHT or ICI, Whether Used Separately or in Combination with ATRA
Given our previous work showing a clinically better response of ERÎ²1-expressing ERÎ±-positive breast tumors to AET and, conversely, increased late recurrence rate of ERÎ±/ERÎ²2-expressing tumors [30], we assessed the response of MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells to OHT and ICI. Figure 2A shows the doseâ€“response effect of OHT on estradiol (E2)-dependent proliferation of MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells. Interestingly, OHT inhibited proliferation of MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells with significantly higher potency and efficacy than that of MCF7-WT and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells (Table 1), demonstrating that ERÎ²1 sensitized MCF7 cells to OHT. Considering that the maximum serum concentration (Cmax) of OHT is 0.11 Î¼Îœ [34], full suppression of cell proliferation at the Cmax is apparently possible for MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells but not for MCF7-WT cells or MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells (Figure 2A). The doseâ€“response effect of ICI on the E2-dependent proliferation of MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells revealed that ICI inhibited proliferation of ERÎ²1- and ERÎ²2-expressing cells with significantly higher and lower potency, respectively, than that of WT cells (Figure 2B and Table 1), demonstrating that ERÎ²1 sensitizes and ERÎ²2 desensitizes MCF7 cells to ICI. Additionally, the efficacy of the antiproliferative effect of ICI was explicitly lower in ERÎ²2-expressing cells as compared to MCF7-WT cells. Full suppression of cell proliferation at the Cmax of ICI (0.04 Î¼Îœ; [34]) was apparently possible for WT and ERÎ²1-expressing cells but not for ERÎ²2-expressing cells (Figure 2B). The antiproliferative effect of pharmacologically relevant concentrations of ICI (30 nM) and OHT (300 nM) was further investigated. Significantly higher efficacy of OHT was observed for MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells, and tentatively lower efficacy was observed for MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells as compared to MCF7-WT cells (Figure 2C). In addition, ICI inhibited proliferation of all cells lines more effectively than OHT, with the efficacy for MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells being significantly higher and lower, respectively, as compared to MCF7-WT cells.

Finally, in order to gain more insight into the joint effects of ATRA and antiestrogens on MCF7 cell proliferation, we investigated the antiproliferative effect of the combination of ATRA (100 nM) with OHT (1 nM) or ICI (1 nM). The selected concentrations for each compound alone caused partial inhibition of E2-dependent cell proliferation (Figure 2A,B and Figure 3A). Figure 3B,C show that the antiproliferative efficacy of OHT+ATRA and ICI+ATRA significantly exceeded that of OHT, ICI and ATRA acting as single agents in MCF7-WT cells; that the antiproliferative efficacy of OHT, ICI, ATRA and the combinations of OHT+ATRA and ICI+ATRA was significantly higher in MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells compared to MCF7-WT cells; and that the antiproliferative efficacy of OHT+ATRA and ICI+ATRA in ERÎ²1-expressing cells significantly exceeded that of OHT, ICI and ATRA acting as single agents. In contrast, the antiproliferative efficacy of OHT, ICI, ATRA and their combinations was significantly lower in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells compared to MCF7-WT cells. In addition, the efficacy of ATRA as single agent in ERÎ²2 cells was significantly lower than that of OHT, ICI and their combination with ATRA. Taken together, the above data show that ERÎ²1 increases and ERÎ²2 decreases the antiproliferative efficacy of ATRA, OHT+ATRA or ICI+ATRA in MCF7 cells.

2.3. OHT, ICI and ATRA Abolished the S-Phase Fraction of ERÎ²1-Expressing MCF7 Cells
Next, we investigated the effect of OHT, ICI, ATRA and their combinations on the cell cycle phase distribution of MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells as assessed by flow cytometry. E2 treatment resulted in a tentatively significant increase in the S-phase fraction of MCF7-WT, whereas it significantly reduced the S-phase fraction of MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells (Figure 4A and Supplementary Table S1). OHT treatment abolished the S-phase fraction of MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells and significantly reduced the S-phase fraction of MCF7-WT cells but not that of MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells. ICI treatment abolished the S-phase fraction of MCF7-WT and MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells and significantly reduced but failed to abolish that of MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells. On the other hand, ATRA alone abolished the S-phase fraction of MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells but did not significantly affect that of MCF7-WT or MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells. When in combination with OHT or ICI, ATRA eliminated the S-phase fraction of MCF7-WT and MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells and significantly reduced but not eliminated that of MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells (Figure 4A and Supplementary Table S1). In short, while the proliferation of ERÎ²1-expressing cells was fully inhibited by OHT at 100 nM (â‰ˆCmax), ATRA at 50 nM (â‰ˆ0.04 Ã— Cmax) and their combination, the proliferation of wild-type cells was fully inhibited only by the combination, and the proliferation of ERÎ²2-expressing cells was inhibited by none of the three treatments. Moreover, in contrast to wild-type and ERÎ²1-expressing cells, the proliferation of ERÎ²2-expressing cells was not inhibited by ICI at 100 nM (=2.5 Ã— Cmax) or its combination with 50 nM ATRA. Of note, the downsizing of the S-phase fraction induced by the combination of ICI and ATRA was accompanied by an increase in the G0/G1-phase fraction that was significant for MCF7-WT cells and tentatively significant for ERÎ²1- and ERÎ²2-expressing cells; however, a tentatively significant increase in the G2/M-phase fraction at the expense of S-phase fraction was observed following treatment of MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells with OHT and ICI (Figure 4A).

2.4. ERÎ²1 Enhanced the Cytocidal Effects of ATRA Combinations with OHT or ICI
To gain insight into the mechanisms underlying the response of ERÎ²1- and ERÎ²2-expressing MCF7 cells to OHT, ICI, ATRA and their combinations, we tested whether these treatments induce cell death. Preliminary experiments revealed that the cell death observed following single-agent or combinatorial treatment was not apoptotic. We therefore resorted to quantifying non-apoptotic cell death by measuring LDH release (lactate dehydrogenase, an enzyme rapidly released in the cell culture supernatant when the plasma membrane is damaged). By taking the release of LDH as a measure of cell death, we show that OHT, ICI and ATRA induced cell death of MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells independently of one another, with ICI exerting the most pronounced effect (Figure 4B). Importantly, the combination of ATRA with OHT or ICI significantly increased cell death in ERÎ²1-expressing cells as compared to similarly treated MCF7-WT cells. In contrast, significantly decreased cell death of ERÎ²2-expressing cells, as compared to similarly treated MCF7-WT cells, was observed upon treatment with the combination of ATRA with OHT. Moreover, the combination of ATRA with OHT significantly increased cell death as compared to OHT alone in all cell lines.

Notably, several reports in the literature suggest that expression of these apoptosis-related proteins is similarly modulated in non-apoptotic as well as in apoptotic cell death. Figure 5A depicts Bax, Bcl-2 and cytochrome c protein levels in MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells upon treatment with OHT, ATRA and OHT+ATRA. 2.5. Global Transcriptional Profile of MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 Cells Treated with OHT+AÎ¤RA
To gain insight into the mechanisms underlying the response of ERÎ²1- and ERÎ²2-expressing MCF7 cells to the combination of OHT and AÎ¤RA, we determined the global transcriptional changes induced upon treatment of MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells with OHT+AÎ¤RA by performing a microarray-based differential gene expression analysis. Our data revealed 245, 294 and 211 differentially expressed genes, as compared to vehicle treatment, in MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells, respectively (Figure 6A and Supplementary Tables S2â€“S4). The microarray data were validated by real time PCR of 10 randomly selected genes differentially expressed in MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells. Supplementary Figure S1 shows that the relative expression levels of the selected genes as computed by the two methodologies are strongly correlated. Comparison of the repertoires of differentially expressed genes showed that approximately 40% are unique for each cell line (Figure 6B), indicating that OHT+AÎ¤RA treatment alters unique as well as common signaling pathways.

3. Discussion
In the present study, we investigated the role of ERÎ² isoforms in the response of breast cancer cells to antiestrogen and retinoid treatment. We established clones of MCF7 cells constitutively expressing human ERÎ²1 or ERÎ²2 and showed that ERÎ²1 and ERÎ²2 sensitizes and desensitizes, respectively, MCF7 cells to the antiproliferative effect of antiestrogens (OHT and ICI), retinoids (ATRA) and their combination. Combination of ATRA with OHT significantly increased and decreased cell death of MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells, respectively, compared to wild-type MCF7 cells. Analysis of the global transcriptional profile of these cells upon treatment with OHT+ATRA revealed uniquely regulated genes that are favorable to ERÎ²1 being a marker of responsiveness and ERÎ²2 being a marker of resistance of MCF7 cells to combinatorial treatment with OHT and ATRA.

The lower levels of ERÎ± mRNA in MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells, as compared to WT cells, are in agreement with previous data reporting ERÎ²1-induced downregulation of ERÎ± gene expression through the ERÎ²1â€“Sp1 interaction and NCoR corepressor recruitment on the ERÎ± promoter [76]. In addition, ERE-dependent transrepression of ERÎ± gene expression by the ERÎ²2/ERÎ± heterodimer, as already reported [24], may account for the lower levels of ERÎ± mRNA in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells as compared to MCF7-WT cells. Transrepression by ERÎ²2 alone may be excluded considering that ERÎ²2 is by itself reportedly unable to modulate ERE-dependent gene transcription [77]. The lower ERÎ± protein levels (that are able to bind estrogen with high affinity) in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells may be, at least in part, due to ERÎ²2-mediated proteasome-dependent degradation of ERÎ± [24].

The reduced induction of transcription of the pS2 gene, an ERE-dependent estrogen-inducible target [31], upon E2 treatment of MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells, as compared to MCF7-WT cells, may be due to the lower ERÎ± levels in these cells. Reduced E2-mediated transcription of the pS2 gene in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells, as compared to MCF7-WT and MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells, also indicates ERE-dependent transrepression of pS2 expression by the ERÎ²2/ERÎ± heterodimer as already reported [24]. Induction of pS2 upon MPP+DPN treatment indicates that ERÎ²1 is transcriptionally active in the absence of ERÎ± activation, which agrees with recent reports [78]. Transcriptional activation by ERÎ²1, either as an ERÎ²1/ERÎ± heterodimer or ERÎ²1 homodimer, could account for the higher E2-mediated pS2 transcription in MCF7-ERÎ²1 as compared to MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells.

We showed that OHT and ICI inhibited the E2-dependent proliferation of MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells with significantly higher potency and efficacy than that of MCF7-WT cells. This finding is in accordance with previous reports on the role of ERÎ²1 in enhancing the antiproliferative efficacy and potency of antiestrogens such as OHT and ICI [79]. Co-expression of ERÎ± and ERÎ²1 appears to support formation of ERÎ±/ERÎ²1 heterodimers of potentially higher antiproliferative potency, which is reportedly dependent on the ligand [80]. Co-expression of ERÎ± and ERÎ²2 in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells resulted in lower efficacy of OHT either due to repression of ERÎ±-mediated transactivation and induction of proteasome-dependent degradation of ERÎ± by ERÎ²2 (as already reported [24]) or due to ERÎ±-independent induction of proliferation by ERÎ²2, as shown for MDA-MB-231 cells [81]. The significantly higher and lower efficacy of OHT in ERÎ±/ERÎ²1- and ERÎ±/ERÎ²2-expressing cells, respectively, compared to cells expressing only ERÎ± could account for the clinically better response of ERÎ²1-expressing ERÎ±-positive breast tumors to endocrine therapy and, conversely, for the increased recurrence rate of ERÎ±/ERÎ²2-expressing tumors [30].

The lower antiproliferative potency and efficacy of ICI in ERÎ²1-expressing cells could be attributed to agonist effects on other modulators, such as the G protein-coupled estrogen receptor (GPER) [82], whereas in ERÎ²2-expressing cells, it may reflect an inherently higher capacity of ERÎ²2 to drive MCF7 cell proliferation in the absence of ICI-degraded ERÎ±, as previously reported for MDA-MB-231 cells [81]. ICI is an ER destabilizer [83] that promotes proteasome-dependent degradation of ERÎ± and ERÎ²1 [79]. ERÎ²2 could increase the basal rate of cell proliferation at pharmacologically relevant concentrations of ICI, which are capable of fully destabilizing ERÎ± but are unable to affect expression and signaling of ERÎ²2 given that the latter is reportedly unable to bind ligands even at relatively high concentrations [22,84]. Persistent ERÎ²2 signaling could account for the late recurrence of ERÎ±/ERÎ²2-expressing tumors [30]. The lower antiproliferative effect of ICI at the Cmax (0.040 Î¼M) [34] in ERÎ±/ERÎ²2-expressing cells compared to ERÎ±/ERÎ²1 cells implies that Faslodex treatment of recurrent ERÎ±-positive tumors that express ERÎ²2 is eventually bound to fail to prevent disease recurrence.

The observed inhibitory effect of ATRA on E2-dependent proliferation of MCF7 cells is in accordance with previous reports on the role of ATRA in promoting inhibition of cell proliferation and enhancement of cell death in MCF7 cells through various mechanisms [13,14,15,16,85]. The higher potency and efficacy of ATRA in MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells could be attributed to ATRA-stimulated RARÎ± interference with cofactor recruitment to the ERÎ²1/ERÎ± heterodimer resulting in enhanced downregulation of ERÎ²1/ERÎ±-dependent cell proliferation as compared to the ERÎ±/ERÎ±-dependent one [7,8,9]. Induction of MCF7 cell proliferation by ERÎ²2, as already reported for MDA-MB-231 cells [81], may account for the lower potency and efficacy of ATRA in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells.

It has been reported that the combination of ATRA and OHT suppressed proliferation and viability of MCF7 cells more than OHT [10,11]. We corroborated these findings and further showed that the combination of ATRA with either OHT or ICI suppressed proliferation and viability of MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells more effectively than those of MCF7-WT cells and that the combination of ATRA with either OHT or ICI suppressed proliferation of MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells less effectively than that of MCF7-WT cells.

The decrease in the S-phase fraction of MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells upon E2 treatment, as compared to MCF7-WT cells, is in agreement with previous reports [79,86]. The difference with the recently reported E2-induced increase in the S-phase fraction in MCF7 cells expressing only ERÎ²1 [78] indicates that the presence of ERÎ± drastically modifies ERÎ²1 signaling to the proliferation machinery of MCF7 cells. In fact, it has been shown that introduction of ERÎ²1 in MCF7 cells inhibited the expression of unique as well as ERÎ±-regulated genes in a manner that favors inhibition of E2-dependent cell proliferation and/or induction of cell death [20]. The abolishment of the S-phase fraction in MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells compared to MCF7-WT cells upon OHT treatment coincides with an increase in G1- and G2/M-phase fractions at the expense of part of the S-phase fraction, as previously reported for OHT-treated ERÎ²1-expressing MCF7 cells [79]. Analysis of our data reveals that the effects of OHT/ICI and/or ATRA on cell cycle phase distributions of MCF7-WT, MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells correlate well with the effects of the respective treatments on cell proliferation and cell death. Notably, the combinations of ATRA with OHT or ICI that exerted the most pronounced antiproliferative and cell death effects in MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells completely abolished the S-phase fraction of these cells but not of MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells.

The observed induction of MCF7 cell death by OHT, ICI or ATRA has been previously reported [85,87]. The significantly increased and decreased cell death by the combination of ATRA with OHT in MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells, respectively, is consistent with the modulation of Bcl-2 levels, the Bax-to-Bcl-2 expression ratio and cytochrome c levels in these cells. The robust downregulation of Bcl-2 expression and increase in the Bax-to-Bcl-2 expression ratio in MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells as well as the downregulation of cytochrome c expression in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells alludes to different mechanisms of cell death depending on ERÎ²1 and ERÎ²2 expression and to the presumption that ERÎ²1 is sensitive and ERÎ²2 insensitive to antiestrogens and the antiestrogen component of combin[<64;83;40Matorial treatments. Downregulation of Bcl-2 expression and an increased Bax-to-Bcl-2 expression ratio may be associated with non-apoptotic cell death in the antiestrogen-responsive MCF7-WT and MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells in agreement with the previously reported induction of non-apoptotic cell death upon treatment of MCF7 cells with OHT and ICI 164 384 [88] and suppression of the pro-survival role of Bcl-2 in ERÎ±-positive breast cancer cells by antiestrogens [89]. In addition, decrease in both cytochrome c and LDH release in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells may suggest suppression of non-apoptotic cell death in these cells in accordance with previously reported data showing that cytochrome c release precedes necrosis as well as apoptosis [90]. The apparently different molecular mechanisms of cell death of MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells warrant further investigation.

Analysis of global transcriptional changes in response to the combination of ATRA with OHT supports our observations regarding cell proliferation and cell death. Downregulation of BCL2 and upregulation of NKX-3, the two unique hub genes in MCF-WT cells, are both consistent with anticancer effects [35,36]. Concerning all the genes that are unique to MCF7-WT cells, the regulation of almost half of them is reportedly associated with anticancer effects, while the regulation of the other half is associated with cancer-promoting effects. Upregulation of five out of the six unique hub genes in MCF7-ERÎ²1 cells reportedly favors inhibition of breast cancer cell proliferation and tumorig[<64;83;40Menicity, induction of cell death and/or better clinical outcome [48,49,50,51,52], which is in accordance with the decreased proliferation and increased cell death observed in response to ATRA+OHT compared to OHT. In contrast, downregulation of CXCL12 and upregulation of SIX4, the two unique hub genes in MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells, are reportedly consistent with cancer-promoting effects and are linked to poor prognosis [65,66]. Regarding all unique genes, 61% of them as regulated in MCF7-ERÎ²1 and MCF7-ERÎ²2 cells are reportedly associated with anti-cancer and cancer-promoting effects, respectively. Our analysis revealed that the five unique hub genes (PPARG, HIPK2, ZFP36L1, HMGB2 and ALDH1A3) may constitute a gene expression signature specifying a therapeutic response of ERÎ²1-expressing ERÎ±-positive breast cancer cells to treatment with OHT+ATRA.
